PCB Checklist

1)Each designator is close to and clearly identifies the associated component
2)All designators are only in one or two orientations
3)Text size and font will remain legible after the fabrications process
4)ICs have pin one clearly marked
5)Board name, print date, and revision number
6)Company logo and product logo (if apply)
7)Warning/Hazard label(s) and Icons (if apply)
8)Mounting points have sufficient clearance for the chosen washer and screw head
9)All components (including mechanical items) have accurate 3D models
10)Tent all vias on your board to avoid possibilities for short circuits
11)Each decoupling capacitor requires its own via for VCC and for GND direct to reference planes
12)Bypass capacitors are placed as close to IC power pins as possible
13)Programmable devices have accessible programming header/pads
14)Verify Existing impedance networks
15)Differential pair tracks lengths are matched
16)The ground plane has sufficient current-carrying vias near connectors, and voltage/return sinks
17)Minimum track widths for all current-carrying nets are sufficient.
18)Repour all polygons
19)Check if an RF shield is required anywhere on the board and if it has a sufficient footprint
20)If you have multiple grounds, ensure that they are tied together at a single point only
21)EMI/RFI filtering is as close to the exit point (board edge, connector, shield) as possible
22)Perform a review of the final assembly drawing files
23)DRC is apply
24)Output files generation are ready
25)Print out the PCB on 1:1 scale and see if special components fit correctly and if it fits the housing
26)Achieve an official “GO” from the customer before going to PCB design
